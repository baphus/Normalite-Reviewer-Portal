In John Dewey's educational philosophy, what is the primary role of the teacher?

expand_more

The Question

In John Dewey's educational philosophy, what is the primary role of the teacher?

cancel Your Answer

A. An authoritative source of absolute knowledge

check_circle Correct Answer

C. A facilitator and guide of student learning experiences

info Rationalization

John Dewey's "Progressivism" emphasizes that education should be student-centered. He believed the teacher should act as a guide or facilitator (partner in learning) rather than a rigid taskmaster or the sole source of wisdom. Students learn best through experience and inquiry.

Which of the following describes the 'Zone of Proximal Development'?

expand_more

The Question

Which of the following describes the 'Zone of Proximal Development' as proposed by Vygotsky?

cancel Your Answer

B. The level of knowledge a child can achieve independently

check_circle Correct Answer

D. The gap between what a learner can do alone and with help

info Rationalization

Lev Vygotsky's ZPD refers to the distance between the actual developmental level as determined by independent problem solving and the level of potential development as determined through problem-solving under adult guidance or in collaboration with more capable peers.
